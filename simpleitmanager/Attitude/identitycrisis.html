Identity Crises

Identity, Security and ID Cards

You are unique, a one-off, there is no-one quite like you in the world. Honestly.

It sounds like a nice compliment, and of course I meant it like that. However, its probably less of a compliment than most of us would first assume, for the rather simple reason that a statement of your uniqueness is blindingly obvious.

That you are a single, unique person, that you have identity is after all kind of obvious. No other human being stands exctly where you are or has lived exactly the same life. There is a singleness about you, one and only one, a uniqueness, or to dip into neology, an identity-ness.

That we are not teribly different from others is the fipside of the compliment. There are millions of people who are as (or more!) intelligent, good-looking and generous. To stand out from the crowd can reinforce your identity-ness, and is probably why fame is so sort after.

So if i am different from everyone else in the world (although maybe not by much) what is all the fuss about. Give us all a card, problem solved.

You have identity, therefore you must have an identity.

The problem is which one?

Imagine a kafka-esque situation for a moment. You wake up one morning and say hello to your husband who demands to know what you are doing in his house. Your kids do not recognise you, your boss and collegues say "who are you". The world has gone mad. Its the beginning of an exciting movie or a fantastically dull book.

But look at things from the point of view of the rest of the world. They remeber a different, possibly better looking person called Susan. They laughed and spent time with her. Now you turn up and say "Hi I am Susan".

The world has two choices - to think that the entire country has undergone some parallel universe swap / mass hypnosis event, or that you are mad, or just lying.

I suspect there are cruel examples of someone suffering total amnesia, and then being told they are someone else entirely. How would an amnesiac know if they were being lied to?

So you do have an identity, but which identity depends on everyone else. The crypto-blokes have a term for this - web of trust.

Essentially it means that the only way to know if you can "trust" person X is to trust the judgement of someone else who also trusts person X. How you arrive at the decision to trust that first someone else is of course up to you. Oh, yes, and how much you trust them is also up to you, but probably a factor of how much you trust the first person.

More corroborations, the better. However the corroboration needs to be qualified with depth of interaction with the claimed identity.

Lets go back to our example of an amnesiac. I can remember no-one - but over time I have learnt to trust my mother. When she corroborates your claim to be my brother, I might be willing to lend you a thousand pounds on the strentgh of that However if I went to a party and 50 people told me "yeah hes your brother, I am sure someone told me just now", I do not think you will get the cash.

In the end our poor amnesiac has a serious problem - ultimately there is no-one he can trust because he cannot remember who he has had deep trust forming relationships, so he cannot trust the judgement of anyone.

SO whats to be done?

Firstly, we accept that a specific identity is not something we have, or are. It is something other people give to us through their interactions with us. Then we realise that authenticating identity is not the point - we need to authenticate our web of trust - the six degrees of seperation f you will.

That biometrics is not really the point - its how much can we trust an ID card. The biometrics are not an authentication - they are an identity. a public declaration. (cf username) so what does a ID card really say - not much actually. it just links one public identity (my retina scan/ face) to another (social security number). Well frankly thats not a lot of help, and it does not need a hundred million pounds.

The problem of authentication is then placed where it should be - with human beings looking at other human beings and going - huh?

The govt does not need to run an ID card system - it needs to run a dispute resolution system, and a large publically available social security and hash photo repository.

The original Identity / Authentication Two-step

I got my first bank account when I was 14 years old - very exciting. Barclays had a "get 'em early" account if my Father would counter sign. No credit, just a savings account. If I wanted to withdraw money, I had to go to the bank and sign a slip.

Now here is the perfect example. I would walk into the bank and announce that I was Paul Brian (14 and 3/4s). The bank teller would not care whether that identity was correct or not. They would just ask me to provide my shared secret - my signature. If my shared secret matched their shared secret, they would give me the cash.

How did they get the shared secret ? They trusted another party (my father) to corroborate that I was Paul Brian, then they would accept a shared secret from me (my signature)
