Tuning your PC to be "Just So"

This might start to get a little nerd-like. Oh hell, a lot nerd-like but I spend the majority of my working life in front of my PC, so it had better act like I want it to act.

We knowledge workers (no laughing at the back there) use computers everyday, but how we use them is a mark of our effectiveness as knowledge workers. I reckon there are four levels at which we can use a computing device [1]

  • Dumb terminal
  • Gadgets and knobs
  • Tool use
  • Tool creation

Dumb terminals I deal with elsewhere.

Gadgets and knobs

This is the screensaver and ringtone level of personalisation. I am using the PC as a gadget, and I dont know or care how to progress further. I fear this is not an uncommon situation. Most computing devices are just gadgets not programming platforms (not withstanding iPhone). And even when they are programming platforms it just seems they are designed to stop people from starting to program.

Tool use

Think photoshop or GIMP here. These, whilst programmable, simply are not used as programming platforms by the users. But they are not by any stretch gadgets - that carries a perjorative that you just cannot use for something as deep and complex as Photoshop. It is a tool, and frankly one that I have considerable admiration for the developers and anyone who can use it more effectively than me (which is probably every six-year old and up)

TOol creation

No, I am not talking about writing you own photoshop, but perhaps a toolchanin ivolving photoshop, or a email procmail or .... Programming involves the things we want to manipulate having a manipulabe part - it is represented in some form in the virtual world, and the knwoledge it can be manipulated, and then the ability to manipulate.

Dumb terminal

I think I am one of my own worst offenders here. Giving "users" dumb terminals is probably the simplest and easiest forms of IT management around. It is cheap(er), easy to implement and reduces the variables people have to play around with, thus reducing my headaches. But I am supposed to be championing the use of programming as a fundamental skill in the 21st Century. So I do this by taking away from people's personal computing. I am not entirely clear I have resolved this, but I make programming available on the terminals, mostly SQL query browser, so I can assuage my guilt.

Whats all this?

This site is a guide to running an IT department or team. It about the stuff that you should obviously know, (but can't quite get) like setting up spam filters on mail servers. Its also about the opinions and attitudes you need, why project management is hard, and why you must read code. And its about other people in the industry, interviews and feedback.

The examples are all aimed at FreeBSD servers, a solid, dependable OS that can be found in many high end server farms.

IT matters

It is a cliche but, computing is going to change the world. And it will take skilled and determined people at the highest reaches of companies and institutions to make that change create better, more human and more interesting places to live and work. I call those people IT Managers.

SoHo From Scratch

Every IT manager should be able to build and run a complete Small Office or Home Office Network, in the same way every chef should be able to prepare a 3 course meal. The fact that many people called IT managers cannot is one of the reasons I have written this site. This area is the more traditional "how to" - a collection of recipies if you will.
