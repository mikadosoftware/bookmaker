computer literacy

Many company's have policy manuals, and process manuals. These explain 'how things are done around here'. They are meant to be read by all the members of the organisation so people know what the process is for say, dealing with the incoming raw materials.

Fortunately the days of process manuals seem to be drawing to a close as companies start to autoamte thier processes - the reasons for doing so are usually to increase the comtrol, repeatibility, monitoring and so forth. [1] All of which can be much more effectively done through software.

The only problem is that I expect most people to be able to read the process manual. They might not actually read it, but put a copy in front of them and eventually they will dip in. Its not the same with software. Even if most people have access to the source code, they are unable to read it.

Most people are computer illiterate.

IT matters

It is a cliche but, computing is going to change the world. And it will take skilled and determined people at the highest reaches of companies and institutions to make that change create better, more human and more interesting places to live and work. I call those people IT Managers.

SoHo From Scratch

Every IT manager should be able to build and run a complete Small Office or Home Office Network, in the same way every chef should be able to prepare a 3 course meal. The fact that many people called IT managers cannot is one of the reasons I have written this site. This area is the more traditional "how to" - a collection of recipies if you will.

Beyond Recipies

Not everything is about configuring systems. Most of the job is configuring people, and the trade secrets of IT management. I give my opinions on everything from project management to out-greening the competition. The youngsters round 'ere think I am a dinosaur.
